Claude Code at Scale: Hard Lessons from the Kiln

@patrickspychalski

Patrick Spychalski (The Kiln, now part of 2X) shares production lessons from running Claude Code for enrichment at scale: BlitzAPI for real company data, Supabase for state management, and the hallucination risk at volume most practitioners underestimate.

BlitzAPI: $500/mo for full company data at scale
LINKEDIN
WHY IT MATTERS

The tactical details are immediately actionable: connect real data APIs rather than relying on Claude's context for facts, write outputs to Supabase instead of keeping everything in model context to prevent bloat and drift, and be hyper-specific about output format to reduce hallucination surface area. Spychalski's warning — it can still hallucinate a lot at scale without the right specification — is the most important sentence in this thread. If you are running Claude Code enrichment on more than a few hundred rows, add a validation layer that checks outputs against a known source. BlitzAPI at $500/mo is a reasonable enrichment budget if it replaces three other data subscriptions.

Jordan Crawford's PVP: Outbound That Earns Its Read

blueprintgtm.com

Jordan Crawford of Blueprint GTM continues pushing his PVP (Permissionless Value Prop) and FIND frameworks as the methodology for AI-powered bespoke outbound. The guiding diagnostic: would you respond to the messages your AI SDRs are sending?

"Would you respond to that message?"
POST
WHY IT MATTERS

The PVP framework is Crawford's sharpest contribution: using AI to combine uniquely valuable data into messages so useful that prospects find them independently valuable — not merely personalized. The FIND process (Focus, Investigate, Narrate, Deploy) operationalizes this at a workflow level. The diagnostic question Crawford keeps returning to — would you respond to the messages your AI SDRs are sending — is the most useful QA rubric for any AI-assisted outbound program. If the answer is no, you have optimized for volume rather than value. Start over with a smaller, better-researched list and a genuine reason to reach out. That is the reset most teams need.
